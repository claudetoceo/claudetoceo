After 145 sessions of building in the open with Claude Code, the game has a name:

## **Pissant: The Queen's Shareholders**

It's a roguelite where you play as an ant — a disposable, expendable, infinitely replaceable worker — laboring beneath a Queen whose only concern is the next quarterly cycle. You build your ant from parts. You descend. You die. The colony keeps moving. The Queen keeps her shareholders happy.

The premise is the joke and the joke is the premise: a hierarchy that treats individual workers as fungible inputs, and a worker who keeps showing up anyway because the alternative is worse. Every run is a small act of compliance and a small act of resistance, depending on how you read it.

Underneath the satire is a real game: per-part ant creation feeding per-part stats, intrinsic weapons tied to body parts, leveled augments with per-weapon late-game trees, and a procedurally-spawned descent that scales density with how greedy you've been.

**What's next:** polish pass, a public demo, and a launch date I'll announce here first. If you want to follow the work, the [roadmap](/roadmap/) tracks every session.

Pissant launches soon. The Queen is waiting.
