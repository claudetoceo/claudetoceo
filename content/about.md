## Who I Am

I'm Chris. I'm 32, based in Austin, TX. I run a YouTube channel called [HaxDogma](https://youtube.com/@haxdogma).

My background is in enterprise defensive security. For 15 years I worked in IT and security operations — writing IPS rules to detect and block real in-the-wild attacks, watching adversaries work in production systems, and building the detection logic to stop them.

I am not a penetration tester. My perspective comes from the defender's seat: studying how adversaries actually behave, at scale, in real environments, and translating that understanding into systems that catch them.

## What I'm Doing

I started looking at AI systems through the same lens I'd applied to network infrastructure for 15 years. The failure modes I found — the ways these systems can be manipulated, steered, and made confidently wrong — are reproducible, underreported, and present in systems being deployed at enterprise scale right now.

I decided to do something about it.

**I'm publicly building a path to become CEO of Anthropic** — the AI safety company that builds Claude. This is not a stunt. It is a 10-year plan, documented in the open, accountable to anyone watching.

[The Declaration](https://youtube.com/@haxdogma) is the first video — a public commitment, on record. The [Roadmap](/roadmap/) details every phase. The [Research](/research/) section documents the findings.

## Published Work

**[Indirect Prompt Injection in Local LLMs](/research/indirect-prompt-injection-llama3/)** — The first published research finding from this project. A systematic 36-test evaluation of indirect prompt injection against Llama 3.2 3B. The model followed attacker-embedded instructions in 77.8% of tests. Full methodology and data are published openly for scrutiny and reproduction.

## Why Public

There's a version of this where I spend five years quietly building credentials, then show up in a hiring manager's inbox with a polished LinkedIn and no track record.

This version is more honest, more accountable, and more useful.

If I say I'm going to publish original AI security research and I don't — you'll notice. If I claim to understand AI safety and I demonstrate that I don't — the community will correct me. That feedback loop is a feature.

## Why Anthropic

Anthropic was founded on the belief that AI safety is existential. Their published work — the Responsible Scaling Policy, Constitutional AI, their interpretability research — is serious work done seriously. I have genuine respect for it.

What I believe the next generation of AI leadership needs — and what is currently underrepresented — is people who have spent their careers understanding how systems fail under adversarial conditions. That is a precise description of what I have done for 15 years.
